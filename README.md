# Zomato Restaurant Analytics Dashboard

## About the Project

This project is an end-to-end restaurant analytics case study built using Python and Power BI. The goal was to explore restaurant data from Zomato and uncover meaningful insights related to customer preferences, pricing trends, restaurant popularity, and overall market behavior.

The project started with data cleaning and exploration in Jupyter Notebook using Python and Pandas. After preparing the data, an interactive Power BI dashboard was created to present the findings in a clear and business-friendly format.

---

## What I Did

* Cleaned and transformed raw restaurant data using Python.
* Handled missing and inconsistent values in ratings and cost columns.
* Converted restaurant pricing data into a usable numeric format.
* Processed cuisine information for better analysis.
* Created restaurant cost segments (Budget, Mid-Range, and Premium).
* Performed exploratory data analysis to identify trends and patterns.
* Designed an interactive three-page Power BI dashboard.

---

## Dashboard Sections

### 1. Market Overview

Provides a high-level view of the restaurant market, including total restaurants, average ratings, average cost, customer votes, location-wise distribution, and cost segment analysis.

### 2. Customer Behavior Analysis

Examines how online ordering and table booking influence customer engagement and restaurant ratings.

### 3. Cuisine & Pricing Analysis

Explores the most popular cuisines, their average ratings, and pricing patterns across the market.

---

## Key Insights

* Budget restaurants make up the largest share of the market.
* BTM and Koramangala are among the most competitive restaurant locations.
* Online ordering has only a small impact on restaurant ratings.
* Restaurants that offer table booking receive significantly higher ratings and customer engagement.
* North Indian and Chinese cuisines dominate the restaurant market.
* Some premium cuisines achieve higher ratings despite having a smaller market presence.
